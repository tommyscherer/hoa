Why Change is Needed

A Call for Change in Our HOA Leadership

Our community deserves leadership that listens, communicates clearly, and acts in the best interest of all homeowners. Several recent decisions have raised serious concerns among residents.

Overreaching Rule Changes

3 new pages of rules added to existing 32-page CCRs without broad community support — introducing aggressive fines, liens, and restrictions on everyday items like plant pots, trash cans, and children's toys.

Financial Transparency Issues

The FY25 budget summary contained errors — inconsistencies in dates and unbalanced debits and credits. HOA dues have increased for the second consecutive year with limited transparency into long-term planning.

Limited Board Participation

An unwillingness to expand board participation — despite unclear bylaws and missing documentation that should define the process for resident involvement.

The new rules, set to take effect April 1st, have already been revised multiple times due to member feedback — and are still changing. This approach is inconsistent with thoughtful, community-driven governance. It's time for a better approach.

Who We're Voting For

Meet the Candidates

Two experienced, community-minded professionals ready to bring balance and common-sense leadership back to Hilton Place.

AM

Andrew Michaels

Candidate for President

Andrew brings 19+ years of leadership experience at Dataworks Development, Inc. (Freezerworks), where he currently serves as Chief Operating Officer. He has progressed from Director of Sales & Support to VP of Customer Relations to COO — leading operations, improving processes, and ensuring strong outcomes throughout.

He has worked closely with federal agencies and research organizations, earning endorsements from professionals such as Larry Pierce of the U.S. Department of Agriculture for his management and collaboration skills.

  • Proven leadership and operational experience at the executive level
  • Strong focus on accountability and organizational transparency
  • Committed to listening to residents and delivering practical solutions
  • Dedicated to keeping the community well-managed and financially responsible
TS

Tommy Scherer

Candidate for Treasurer

Tommy brings over a decade of experience managing complex technical programs, budgets, and customer engagements across commercial and government sectors. He currently serves as Director of Commercial Sales at Defense Unicorns, working with partners and federal organizations to modernize software delivery.

His career spans leadership roles at Spectro Cloud, D2iQ, Diamanti, and Violin Systems — aligning technical solutions with business needs, managing large-scale projects, and supporting high-value contracts.

  • Strong experience managing budgets, forecasts, and high-value programs
  • Clear communicator who translates complex financial topics into plain language
  • Detail-oriented with a track record of accountability and follow-through
  • Committed to responsible financial management and transparent HOA reporting
